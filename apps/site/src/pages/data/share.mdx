Sharing scientific data through BDC helps make approved research data available for access, reuse, and future biomedical discovery.

BDC can be used as a data repository for researchers preparing to submit or share data in accordance with NIH and NHLBI data sharing expectations. This page is for researchers and study teams preparing to make data available through BDC.

*If you want to analyze BDC-hosted data or bring your own data into a workspace for analysis, visit the Analyze Data page.*

## NIH and NHLBI Data Management and Sharing Policies
NIH \- including its institutes, centers, and offices \- has data-sharing policies that researchers must abide by, including the NIH [Data Management and Sharing (DMS) Policy (effective January 25, 2023\)](https://sharing.nih.gov/data-management-and-sharing-policy/about-data-management-and-sharing-policies/data-management-and-sharing-policy-overview#after), which provides information about [selecting a data repository](https://sharing.nih.gov/data-management-and-sharing-policy/sharing-scientific-data/selecting-a-data-repository). 

The [NHLBI Supplement to the NIH Policy for Data Management and Sharing (effective May 25, 2023\)](https://www.nhlbi.nih.gov/grants-and-training/policies-and-guidelines/nhlbi-policy-for-data-sharing) provides additional information for NHLBI-funded research and research that is ancillary to NHLBI-funded studies. It also encourages submission of data into BDC, "especially for data from studies that must comply with [NHLBI's Accrual of Human Subjects (Milestones) Policy](https://www.nhlbi.nih.gov/grants-and-training/policies-and-guidelines/accrual-of-human-subjects-milestones-policy) and for those projects supported by funding opportunity announcements that encourage data deposition into BDC." 

In addition to the policies, researchers may find it helpful to read [NHLBI's Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs) section on Data Repositories](https://www.nhlbi.nih.gov/grants-and-training/data-sharing-policy-faq-data-repositories).

Researchers who plan to use BDC as their data repository should include "NHLBI BioData Catalyst" in their [data management and sharing plans](https://sharing.nih.gov/data-management-and-sharing-policy/planning-and-budgeting-for-data-management-and-sharing/writing-a-data-management-and-sharing-plan#after).

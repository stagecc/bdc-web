**BDC collaborates with research programs, fellowship and training initiatives, NIH cloud ecosystem partners, and scientific communities to support secure data sharing, cloud-based analysis, interoperability, and biomedical discovery.**

This page highlights selected BDC collaborations and is not an exhaustive list.

The following are some examples of key BDC collaborations. This page highlights selected collaborations and is not an exhaustive list of BDC’s history.

## Fellowship Collaborations

BDC has supported fellowship and training efforts that help researchers use BDC data, tools, and cloud resources. From March 2020 through February 2022, the NHLBI BDC Fellows Program supported early-career researchers who provided feedback that helped inform development of the BDC ecosystem.

Selected fellowship-related collaborations and references are listed below.

### BDC Fellows Program

From March 2020 through February 2022, the NHLBI BDC Fellows Program supported over 45 fellows who advanced scientific research using BDC and contributed feedback that informed ecosystem development. The Fellows also contributed towards some of the first BDC-Enabled Research.

<Button to="/about/bdc/fellows" centered>View the NHLBI BDC Fellows</Button>

### AIM-AHEAD Research Fellowship

The [AIM-AHEAD Research Fellowship](https://www.aim-ahead.net/programs/research-fellowship-program/) names BDC as a "dataset option" early-career researcher applicants from under-represented populations can use to actively participate in biomedical research that involves the use of artificial intelligence/machine learning methodologies.

### TOPMed Fellowship Program

The [NHLBI Trans-Omics for Precision Medicine (TOPMed) Fellowship Program](https://topmed.nhlbi.nih.gov/) provides training to early-career investigators, leveraging the TOPMed datasets and the BDC computing environment to conduct impactful precision medicine research.

## BDC for Courses and Academic Training

### BDC for Courses

BDC supports hands-on learning and helps prepare the next generation of researchers to work with data in secure environments. It is used in courses, workshops, and academic training sessions at universities and research institutions to help students and early-career researchers learn about cloud computing. In these settings, BDC is typically used less as a research environment and more as a teaching platform for concepts such as cloud computing, genome-wide association studies, RNA-seq analysis, and reproducible research workflows.

<Button centered>Learn About Using BDC in an Academic Setting</Button>

### SISG

BDC-Seven Bridges has been used in the Bruce Weir Summer Institute in Statistical Genetics (SISG) Whole Genome Sequence Analysis Pipeline module to support hands-on instruction in cloud-based whole-genome sequence analysis workflows. Students learn how cloud ecosystems can support large-scale data analysis in a practical instructional setting.

## National Institutes of Health (NIH) Cloud Ecosystem Collaborations

BDC also partners with others in the NIH cloud computing domain to help build its community of practice and accelerate progress in the data and biomedical community.

### NCPI

The [NIH Cloud Platform Interoperability Effort (NCPI)](https://datascience.nih.gov/nih-cloud-platform-interoperability-effort) establishes and implements guidelines and technical standards to empower end-user analyses across participating platforms and facilitate the realization of a trans-NIH, federated data ecosystem. BDC is participating in the NCPI \- along with AnVIL, the Cancer Research Data Commons, and the Kids First Data Resource Center \- to enable cross-platform data sharing and analysis.

### STRIDES

The [NIH Science and Technology Research Infrastructure for Discovery, Experimentation, and Sustainability (STRIDES)](https://datascience.nih.gov/strides) Initiative allows NIH to provide cost-effective access to industry-leading partners to help advance biomedical research. These partnerships enable access to rich datasets and advanced computational infrastructure, tools, and services. BDC leverages STRIDES to enhance the efficiency of data storage and computation.

### ODSS

The [NIH Office of Data Science Strategy (ODSS)](https://datascience.nih.gov/data-infrastructure) supports efficient and effective biomedical research data infrastructure to achieve NIH's mission of applying knowledge gained through research to improve health. BDC works with ODSS to facilitate secure data storage and broad access to biomedical datasets.

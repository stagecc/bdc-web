[Researching COVID to Enhance Recovery (RECOVER)](https://recovercovid.org/) is a first-of-its-kind, patient-centered research initiative to understand, diagnose, and treat Long COVID. RECOVER research includes observational cohort studies, electronic health records analyses, pathobiology studies, tissue pathology studies, and clinical trials.

RECOVER studies involve thousands of people from all walks of life, hundreds of research investigators, and millions of electronic health records (EHRs). RECOVER aims to achieve the following:

* Understand the range of symptoms and impacts on quality of life for individuals experiencing Long COVID.  
* Define risk factors and causes associated with Long COVID.  
* Study how Long COVID changes over time and how those changes may relate to other illnesses.  
* Identify possible treatments for Long COVID symptoms.

The Data References and Access Information section on the RECOVER [Data](https://recovercovid.org/data) page provides access to key resources for working with RECOVER data. These resources include Data Dictionaries, Data Release Notes, and Data Use Agreements for Adult/Pregnancy, Pediatric, and Tissue Pathology datasets. There are also additional Data Access Resources that support data access requests, data sharing compliance, and use of RECOVER datasets.
